Louis Pasteur

Biography

Louis Pasteur was a French chemist and microbiologist born on December 27, 1822, in Dole, France. He is renowned for his discoveries of the principles of vaccination, microbial fermentation, and pasteurization.

Key Contributions

  • Developed the germ theory of disease.
  • Created vaccines for rabies and anthrax.
  • Invented the process of pasteurization to reduce spoilage of beverages.
